Duterte administration launches "Build, Build, Build" program

On July 4, 2017, President Rodrigo Duterte's administration launched the "Build, Build, Build" program, a massive infrastructure plan aimed at modernizing the country's roads, bridges, airports, and seaports. The program aims to boost economic growth and create more jobs for Filipinos.

The program includes 75 flagship projects, with a total cost of around PHP 8.4 trillion (USD 168 billion). Some of the major projects under the program include the construction of a new international airport in Bulacan, a new terminal building for the Clark International Airport, and the Metro Manila Subway.

Critics have raised concerns over the high cost of the program and its potential impact on the country's debt. However, the government has defended the program, stating that it is necessary to address the country's infrastructure backlog.

President Duterte signs Universal Health Care law

On February 20, 2019, President Duterte signed the Universal Health Care law, which aims to provide all Filipinos with access to quality health care services. Under the law, all Filipinos will be automatically enrolled in the government's health insurance program, PhilHealth.

The law also mandates the establishment of a network of primary care facilities and the hiring of more health care professionals, among other measures. The government has allocated PHP 257 billion (USD 5 billion) for the implementation of the law.

The signing of the law was hailed by health care advocates and experts, who have long been calling for a more comprehensive and accessible health care system in the country.
